Quality Policy

Shri Karni Group of Companies is committed to delivering electrical and civil infrastructure works that consistently meet or exceed the requirements of our clients, regulatory authorities, and applicable standards including AAI, CPWD, IRC, and IS specifications.

  • In-house testing and quality assurance at every stage of construction

Environmental Policy

[Replace with the actual environmental policy from the company profile. Below is a placeholder structure.]

Shri Karni Group of Companies recognises its responsibility to the environment and is committed to conducting all operations in a manner that minimises adverse environmental impact, complies with all applicable environmental legislation, and continuously improves our environmental performance.

Our commitments include:

  • Minimising waste generation and ensuring responsible disposal of construction debris
  • Controlling dust, noise, and vibration at construction sites to protect surrounding communities
  • Preventing pollution of soil, water, and air through proper management of fuel, oils, and chemicals
  • Using resources — water, energy, and materials — efficiently and responsibly
  • Complying fully with all environmental clearances, NOCs, and statutory requirements applicable to our projects
  • Training all site staff on environmental responsibilities and reporting procedures

This policy is implemented through our Environmental Management System and reviewed at least annually by senior management.

Health & Safety Policy

[Replace with the actual health and safety policy from the company profile. Below is a placeholder structure.]

The health, safety, and welfare of our employees, subcontractors, clients, and the public is of paramount importance to Shri Karni Group. We are committed to achieving the highest standards of occupational health and safety across all our operations.

Our commitments include:

  • Providing a safe working environment, safe plant and equipment, and safe systems of work
  • Conducting risk assessments and method statements for every site activity, especially airside works
  • Ensuring all personnel are appropriately trained, qualified, and equipped with personal protective equipment
  • Complying with all DGCA airside safety requirements, OHSAS 18001 standards, and applicable Indian labour laws
  • Promptly investigating all incidents, near-misses, and unsafe conditions, and implementing corrective actions
  • Maintaining a culture where every employee is empowered and expected to stop unsafe work
  • Conducting regular safety inspections, toolbox talks, and emergency drills

This policy is reviewed annually and its implementation is the personal responsibility of every manager and supervisor in the organisation.

Signed: [Director Name], Managing Director  |  Date: [Policy Date]
